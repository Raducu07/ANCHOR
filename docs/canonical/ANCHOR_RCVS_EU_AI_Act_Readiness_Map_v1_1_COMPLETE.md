# 1. Positioning and scope

ANCHOR helps clinics evidence responsible AI governance practices aligned with emerging professional and regulatory expectations. v1.1 keeps v1's claim boundary but updates it from pre-build planning to as-built evidence mapping.

## 1.1 What ANCHOR is
Governance, trust, learning, intelligence, and readiness infrastructure for safe AI use in veterinary clinics. Metadata-only by default. Receipt-backed. Human-review based. Multi-tenant and privacy-aware. Trust-surface oriented. Standalone now, integrable later.

## 1.2 What ANCHOR is not
- Not a diagnostic, prescribing, treatment-planning, or autonomous triage system.
- Not an ambient scribe or EHR.
- Not a clinical decision-support product.
- Not a GPAI model provider.
- Not a compliance guarantee, certification, or regulator-approved product.
- Not a replacement for veterinary judgement.

## 1.3 ANCHOR's GPAI non-position
ANCHOR is not a provider of a general-purpose AI model. It is downstream governance infrastructure around AI tools used by clinics. The current live Workspace generation path is Anthropic-coupled and production-off; Anthropic becomes a subprocessor only when live generation is enabled. Public wording must say architected for vendor-neutrality or vendor-neutral over time, not present-tense vendor-neutrality.

## 1.4 ANCHOR's high-risk non-classification
ANCHOR is not positioned as a high-risk AI system under EU AI Act Annex III. Its doctrine prohibits diagnosis, prescribing, treatment planning, autonomous triage, and autonomous clinical decision-making. The product governs around AI use; it is not the clinical AI being governed.

## 1.5 UK jurisdiction note
ANCHOR is UK-first. UK clinics are not automatically subject to every EU AI Act obligation unless they operate in the EU, provide or deploy systems whose output is used in the EU, or otherwise fall within scope. EU AI Act readiness remains commercially valuable for EU-facing clinic groups, insurers with EU exposure, education providers, and future EU expansion. Legal applicability requires qualified advice.

## 1.6 Article 4 position after source check
Article 4 entered into application on 2 February 2025. For Readiness Map v1.1, supervision/enforcement timing should be expressed as from August 2026 because official Commission material carries both 2 August and 3 August phrasings for supervision/enforcement. Commission Q&A says there is no need for a certificate and organisations can keep internal records of training/guidance initiatives. Digital Omnibus must be tracked because the Commission proposed shifting Article 4 toward Member State/Commission promotion of AI literacy rather than enforcing an unspecific organisational obligation, while high-risk deployer training for human oversight remains. ANCHOR therefore leans on RCVS professional expectations and good-governance conviction, not on a hard Article 4 legal stick.

## 1.7 Release-candidate gate position
No buyer-conversation gate and no parallel buyer-discovery requirement. Paid pilots or real clinic data require security audit, operational resilience, and legal/commercial pack with solicitor review. Live generation remains production-off until the local/staging safety gate passes.
