## 3 — Article 4 — the operative wording for ANCHOR

Article 4 is the single highest-leverage obligation for ANCHOR's Phase 2A-1 CPD-Recordable AI Literacy feature. Two facts to anchor on:

1. **Applicable since 2 February 2025** (per Article 113).
2. **National market surveillance authority enforcement powers activate 2 August 2026** (per Article 113).

The substantive obligation is on Article 4: providers and deployers of AI systems must take measures, to their best extent, to ensure a sufficient level of AI literacy for their staff and other persons dealing with AI on their behalf, taking account of technical knowledge, experience, education and training, the context in which the AI systems are used, and the persons or groups of persons on whom the AI systems are used.

For exact wording, refer to the EUR-Lex text of Article 4. Do not paraphrase into stronger language. Do not paraphrase into ANCHOR marketing copy without checking the wording controls in Readiness Map §2.

Recital 20 of the Regulation provides the AI-literacy context. Cite by recital number if cited at all; do not summarise loosely.

## 5 — Citation discipline for ANCHOR documents

When any ANCHOR artefact (roadmap, readiness map, decision memo, engineering brief, public site copy, sales deck, Trust Pack material) references the EU AI Act:

1. Cite by **Article number** and **Regulation (EU) 2024/1689**, not by colloquial name.
2. For applicability dates, cite **Article 113** as the source of the date.
3. Link to the **ELI** (`http://data.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj`) or the English HTML version on EUR-Lex.
4. Do not cite third-party explainers, law-firm blog posts, or consultancy summaries as primary sources. They may be used as background reading; they are not the source.
5. Do not paraphrase Article text into stronger claims. If exact wording matters, quote the EUR-Lex text in a short, attributed excerpt.
