# Data Handling When Using AI

**Status:** Template stub v1.0.0

This ANCHOR template is a governance support artefact for clinic review and adaptation. It is not legal advice, regulatory approval, clinical guidance, or proof of compliance. Adopting clinics remain responsible for the final policy that applies to their practice.

## Purpose

This template provides a starting point for what client and patient information may or may not be entered into AI tools, and the clinic's expectations around retention, sharing, and confidentiality when using those tools.

## Principles (to adapt)

- The clinic remains responsible for the lawful basis and confidentiality of any data it processes, including via AI tools.
- Identifiable client or patient information should be handled in line with the clinic's wider data protection posture, not loosened because an AI tool is in use.
- AI tool selection and configuration should reflect the clinic's data handling expectations, not the other way round.

## Scope (to adapt)

- Which categories of information are acceptable inputs to AI tools.
- Which categories are not.
- Expectations around retention of inputs and outputs by the AI tool vendor.
- How staff should escalate uncertainty about a specific case.

## Out of scope

This template does not provide a data protection impact assessment, a lawful-basis assessment, or a vendor due-diligence checklist.

## Notes

Template content is to be developed and reviewed by the clinic before external use. ANCHOR does not certify, approve, or endorse any policy adopted from this template.
