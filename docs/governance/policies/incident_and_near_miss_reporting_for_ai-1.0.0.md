# Incident and Near-Miss Reporting for AI Use

**Status:** Template stub v1.0.0

This ANCHOR template is a governance support artefact for clinic review and adaptation. It is not legal advice, regulatory approval, clinical guidance, or proof of compliance. Adopting clinics remain responsible for the final policy that applies to their practice.

## Purpose

This template provides a starting point for how clinic staff should record and report AI-related incidents and near-misses, so the clinic can review patterns over time and adjust its use of AI tools.

## Principles (to adapt)

- Reporting is a learning mechanism, not a punitive one.
- Near-misses are valuable evidence and should be recorded even when no harm occurred.
- Records are governance metadata; they are not a substitute for any external regulatory reporting obligation that the clinic may have.

## Scope (to adapt)

- Which categories of AI behaviour are in scope (for example: clearly wrong output, unexpected refusal, content the reviewer disagreed with, output that would have been harmful if not caught).
- Who records the event and where.
- How records are reviewed and by whom.

## Out of scope

This template does not establish a clinical incident management system and does not constitute legal or regulatory reporting guidance.

## Notes

Template content is to be developed and reviewed by the clinic before external use. ANCHOR does not certify, approve, or endorse any policy adopted from this template.
